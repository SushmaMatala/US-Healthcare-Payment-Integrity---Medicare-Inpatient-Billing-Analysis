# 🏥 US Healthcare Payment Integrity — Medicare Inpatient Billing Analysis

> **CMS Medicare Inpatient Dataset | 2017–2018 | 388,155 records | 3,182 hospitals**

A rigorous, end-to-end data analysis of Medicare inpatient billing gaps using real CMS public datasets. This project investigates how much hospitals charge vs. what Medicare actually pays — and whether hospital ownership type (For-Profit vs. Non-Profit vs. Government) drives structural differences in billing behaviour.

---

## 📌 Key Findings

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Median charge ratio (billed ÷ paid) | **4.42×** |
| Mean charge ratio | **4.95×** |
| Max charge ratio | **18.94×** — Capital Health Medical Center, Hopewell NJ |
| Hospitals billing ≥ 6× Medicare payment | **25.6%** |
| Hospitals billing ≥ 10× Medicare payment | **5.6%** |
| Top MDC by Medicare spend | **MDC 05 Circulatory — $41.73B** |
| Highest charge-ratio state (Circulatory) | **Florida — 7.60×** |
| For-Profit billing premium over Non-Profit | **+50.2%** (6.41× vs 4.27×) |
| Mann-Whitney U p-value (FP vs NP) | **5.35 × 10⁻⁶³** |
| For-Profit share of top-100 outlier hospitals | **72.0%** vs 19.6% of all hospitals |

### 🔑 Own Finding — The For-Profit Billing Premium is Structural, Not Clinical

For-Profit hospitals bill **+50.2% more** than Non-Profits at **every DRG complexity decile (D1–D10)**. The premium is flat across simple and complex cases, ruling out case-mix as an explanation. This is a structural/ownership-driven phenomenon, statistically confirmed at p = 5.35 × 10⁻⁶³.

## 🔬 Analysis Sections

### Section 1 — Data Loading & Preparation
- Loaded and merged three CMS tables (388,155 rows across 2 years)
- Standardised provider CCN (6-digit) and DRG codes (3-digit zero-padded)
- Mapped MS-DRGs to Major Diagnostic Categories (MDCs) via numeric range rules
- Computed `billing_gap = avg_submitted_charge − avg_medicare_payment` and `charge_ratio = submitted ÷ medicare`

### Section 2 — Billing Gap Analysis
- Distribution of charge ratios across 3,058 hospitals (median 4.42×, skewness 1.32)
- Geographic deep-dive on MDC 05 Circulatory ($41.73B Medicare spend): Florida highest CR, California highest absolute payment
- Identified extreme outliers: 5.6% of hospitals bill >10× Medicare rates

### Section 3 — Ownership, Case Mix & DRG Complexity
- For-Profit median CR: **6.41×** vs Non-Profit: **4.27×** vs Govt: **1.96–3.41×**
- MDC case-mix heatmap: near-identical disease mix across ownership types — rules out patient acuity as driver
- DRG weight vs payment scatter: all ownership groups show similar (low) sensitivity to case complexity

### Section 4 — Own Finding: The For-Profit Billing Premium
- Decile analysis (D1–D10 by DRG relative weight): For-Profit premium is flat across all complexity levels
- Mann-Whitney U test: p = 5.35 × 10⁻⁶³ — statistically definitive
- 72% of top-100 outlier hospitals are For-Profit, despite representing only ~19.6% of the hospital population
